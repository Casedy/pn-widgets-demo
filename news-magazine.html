USA POLITICS

Biden Meets with Congressional Leaders to Avert Government Shutdown

WASHINGTON - President Joe Biden convened an emergency meeting with congressional leaders from both parties today in an effort to break the impasse over government funding ahead of Friday's shutdown deadline. Negotiations continue as lawmakers debate spending levels for defense and domestic programs.

"We need to put politics aside and do what's right for the American people," Biden told reporters before entering the Oval Office meeting.

TECHNOLOGY

NASA Announces New Moon Mission Partners

CAPE CANAVERAL - NASA has selected three private companies to develop lunar landers for future Artemis missions. This move signals increased collaboration between government and commercial space sectors in the new space race.

CHINA ECONOMY

Beijing Unveils New Stimulus Package to Boost Slowing Economy

BEIJING - Chinese policymakers announced a comprehensive 500 billion yuan ($68 billion) economic stimulus package today, focusing on tax cuts for small businesses and infrastructure spending. This comes amid concerns about slowing growth and the property market crisis.

DIPLOMACY

China-US Relations: Trade Talks Resume After 6-Month Hiatus

SHANGHAI - Senior trade officials from the United States and China began new round of negotiations today, focusing on technology transfers and tariffs. Both sides expressed cautious optimism but acknowledged significant differences remain.

"We're committed to finding solutions that benefit both nations," said Chinese lead negotiator Vice Premier Liu He.
